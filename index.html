What Is a Button Cell Battery?

A button cell battery, also known as a coin cell battery, is a small, round, and flat single-cell battery typically used in compact electronic devices that require a low and consistent power supply. The name "button cell" comes from its button-like shape, while "coin cell" emphasizes its similarity in size and shape to coins.

Physical Characteristics

  • Shape: Round, flat
  • Size: 5 mm to 25 mm in diameter; 1 mm to 6 mm thick
  • Voltage: 1.5V to 3V depending on chemistry
  • Capacity: Low mAh, suited for low-drain applications
  • Polarity: Flat side is positive; rimmed side is negative

Chemical Compositions

Chemistry Type Voltage Characteristics Typical Applications
Alkaline 1.5V Low cost, moderate capacity, may leak over time Toys, laser pointers, calculators
Silver Oxide 1.55V Stable voltage, longer life, higher cost Watches, medical devices, hearing aids
Lithium (MnO₂) 3V High energy density, long shelf life Key fobs, remote controls, motherboards
Zinc-Air 1.4V Air activated, high capacity Hearing aids

How Does a Button Cell Work?

Button cells generate electricity through an electrochemical reaction between the anode (negative) and cathode (positive), with an electrolyte in between. The chemistry determines voltage and performance. Typical components include:

  • Anode: Zinc or Lithium
  • Cathode: Silver oxide, manganese dioxide, or air cathode
  • Electrolyte: KOH (alkaline), organic solvent (lithium), salt water (zinc-air)

Common Applications

  • Wristwatches
  • Hearing aids
  • Digital thermometers
  • Laser pointers
  • Calculators
  • Car remotes
  • Motherboards (BIOS)
  • LED keychains and toys

Button Cell vs. Regular Batteries

Feature Button Cell AA/AAA Battery
Size Small and flat Larger cylindrical
Voltage 1.5V – 3V 1.5V (usually)
Capacity Low High
Rechargeable Mostly non-rechargeable Many rechargeable types available
Use Case Small electronics General-purpose electronics

Environmental Notes

Older button cells often contained mercury, which is hazardous. Today, most are mercury-free and environmentally safer. Proper recycling is essential to prevent leakage and pollution.

Summary

Button cell batteries are essential compact power sources for many low-power electronic devices. With a wide range of chemistries, voltages, and sizes, understanding their characteristics helps in choosing the right battery for the right application.
